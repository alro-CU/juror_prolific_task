Purpose of the Study

You are being asked to take part in a research study at University of Colorado Boulder to study how people make decisions. The purpose of this study is to help us learn more about how juries make decisions. We expect about 85 people will be in this research study. Your participation in this study will take approximately 30 minutes. You may choose to end your participation at any time. In order to receive compensation, however, you must complete the entire survey.

Explanation of Procedures
In this study, you will make a series of decisions. You may also evaluate a series of scenarios using various ratings for case strength and other measures. Some of the decisions may involve how you feel about certain people in a story. You may be asked to compare different characteristics and weigh their importance for a jury. There are no right or wrong answers in these decisions -- you are free to choose whatever best matches your opinions, personal beliefs, or preferences.

The experiment will be conducted on a computer, and your decisions will be recorded using a mouse and keyboard. During the experiment, you will be presented with questions asking about attitudes or preferences related to decision making. The questionnaires may ask for some personal or sensitive information, and if you do not wish to answer any question you are not obligated to do so.

Some of the stories in this experiment may involve the possibility of an imaginary crime. Some of these crimes are more or less serious than others. A few may involve homicide or sexual assault. There are no graphic details in any of the stories, and all of the stories discuss the matter in a factual manner.

Voluntary Participation and withdrawal
Whether or not you take part in this research is your choice. You can leave the research at any time and it will not be held against you.

During the task, you will be periodically asked simple comprehension questions regarding the scenarios you read. If you answer more than three of these incorrectly, the survey will end and you will be automatically withdrawn from the study. Data from participants who either choose to withdraw or are withdrawn as a result of answering the comprehension questions incorrectly will be discarded.

Risks and Discomforts
There are no health risks associated with this study. None of the stimuli used in this experiment are threatening, offensive, or stressful to an average person.

Confidentiality
Information obtained about you for this study will be kept confidential to the extent allowed by law. Research information that identifies you may be shared with the University of Colorado Boulder Institutional Review Board (IRB) and others who are responsible for ensuring compliance with laws and regulations related to research, including people on behalf of the Office for Human Research Protections. The information from this research may be published for scientific purposes; however, your identity will not be given out.

To protect your confidentiality, your data will be kept on password-protected computers, each of which will only be accessible to investigators on this project. Your data will be coded by an arbitrary subject number that is stored separately from the consent form. This information will never be connected to your study data.

Payment for Participation
You will be paid the specified amount indicated by the Prolific system for your online study. Payments will be mediated through Prolific upon appropriate completion of the task as determined by the investigator.
