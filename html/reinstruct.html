We have new information about the remaining scenarios:

Both the defense and the prosecution conducted thorough investigations of each case, and all of the evidence found by both sides will be shown.

No evidence from either side was ommitted or ruled inadmissable in any of the following scenarios. This means that if the prosecution presents little or no evidence of the defendent's guilt, then the prosecution had little to no evidence for the original criminal charge.

Similarly, if the defense presents no evidence, then the defense did not find any evidence against the criminal charge.
